Why is UDP Stop-and-Wait slower than TCP?
TCP pipelines data continuously — the OS manages flow control internally. Our UDP protocol with Stop-and-Wait sends one packet, waits for an ACK, then sends the next. On loopback, RTT ≈ 0.1 ms but we still pay a full round-trip per packet. Enable Sliding Window (window ≥ 8) in the sidebar to see throughput approach TCP levels.
